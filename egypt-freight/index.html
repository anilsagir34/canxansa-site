ALEXANDRIA, PORT SAID & THE SUEZ CANAL — EGYPT'S EXPORT GATEWAYS

Egypt's foreign trade runs through a unique geography: Alexandria, the historic Mediterranean container port; Port Said and Damietta at the northern mouth of the Suez Canal; and Ain Sokhna on the Red Sea, the natural loading point for Gulf and Asia routings. Together with Cairo International Airport, they place Egyptian cargo at the crossroads of Europe, the Middle East and Asia — with the Suez Canal carrying a large share of world trade past Egypt's own doorstep.

From Egypt, sea freight reaches Turkey in 4–7 days across the eastern Mediterranean — one of the fastest sea corridors in the region — Europe in 6–14 days, the Gulf in 3–11 days via the Red Sea and the Far East in 22–28 days; air cargo from Cairo reaches Istanbul and the European hubs in about a day; and RoRo links Egyptian ports with Mersin and İskenderun for onward road haulage into Turkey and Europe. Following the normalization of Turkish–Egyptian relations, bilateral trade has grown strongly. Canxansa manages end-to-end shipments — customs, freight and last-mile delivery under a single point of contact.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
EGYPT — FAQ.

What is the fastest way to ship from Egypt to Turkey and Europe?

Air freight from Cairo International (CAI) reaches Istanbul in about a day and the main European hubs in roughly a day door-to-door, ideal for high-value and time-critical cargo. For volume, sea freight is the most economical mode: Egypt to Turkey (Mersin) runs about 4–7 days across the eastern Mediterranean — one of the fastest sea corridors in the region — and to North Europe (Rotterdam, Hamburg) 10–14 days. Transit times are indicative and depend on the carrier's service loop and routing.

How long does sea freight from Egypt take?

Indicative from Alexandria / Port Said / Damietta: Egypt to Turkey (Mersin) 4–7 days; Egypt to Italy (Genoa) 6–9 days; Egypt to Spain and France 5–8 days; Egypt to North Europe 10–14 days; Egypt to Saudi Arabia (Jeddah) 3–6 days via the Red Sea; Egypt to the UAE (Jebel Ali) 7–11 days; Egypt to India 10–14 days; and Egypt to China 22–28 days via the Suez Canal and Red Sea. FCL (20'/40') and LCL groupage are both available. LCL adds 3–5 days for consolidation and deconsolidation.

Which ports and airports do you work with in Egypt?

Sea: Alexandria (EGALY), Egypt's main commercial port, plus Port Said (EGPSD) at the northern mouth of the Suez Canal, Damietta (EGDAM) for containers and Ain Sokhna (EGSOK) on the Red Sea for Gulf and Asia routings. Air: Cairo International (CAI) and Borg El Arab / Alexandria (HBE). We arrange pickup across Egypt, including Cairo, Alexandria, the Delta and the industrial zones.

Can you route Egyptian freight to the Gulf and Asia via the Suez Canal?

Yes. Egypt sits astride the Suez Canal, so cargo for Saudi Arabia, the UAE, India and the Far East moves directly via the Red Sea — often loading at Ain Sokhna or transhipping at Port Said. Egypt to Jeddah runs about 3–6 days, to Jebel Ali 7–11 days and to the Far East 22–28 days. This geographic position makes Egypt one of the most flexible origins for both European and Asian destinations.

Do you handle road and RoRo freight between Egypt and Turkey?

Yes. RoRo and ferry services link Egyptian ports with Mersin and İskenderun in Turkey, carrying trucks and trailers across the Mediterranean and onward into Turkey and Europe by road. This is well-suited to wheeled, palletised and project cargo. We also arrange regional road freight within Egypt and to neighbouring markets.

What export documents does an Egyptian exporter need?

Standard documents: commercial invoice, packing list, the transport document — bill of lading (sea) or airway bill (air) — the Egyptian customs export declaration, and a certificate of origin attested by the relevant chamber where the destination requires it. Some categories such as food and agricultural produce need health or phytosanitary certificates. We advise on full documentation before shipment.

What goods are commonly exported from Egypt?

Egypt's export base includes fertilizers and chemicals, ceramics, marble and granite, fresh produce (citrus, onions, potatoes), textiles and cotton, processed food, and petroleum and LNG products. High-value and perishable lines move by air, while fertilizers, ceramics, stone and general cargo move by sea FCL and breakbulk from Alexandria, Port Said and Damietta.
