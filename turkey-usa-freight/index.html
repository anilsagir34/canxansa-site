Common Questions

TURKEY → USA
FREIGHT FAQ.

How long does sea freight from Turkey to the USA take?

FCL sea freight from Istanbul or Mersin to US East Coast ports (New York, Savannah) typically takes 18–24 days. West Coast ports (Los Angeles, Long Beach) take 26–32 days. LCL groupage adds a few days for consolidation and de-consolidation at either end.

What US ports can you ship to from Turkey?

We ship to all major US ports — New York/New Jersey, Savannah, Baltimore and Charleston on the East Coast; Los Angeles, Long Beach and Seattle on the West Coast; and Houston and Miami for Gulf and Southeast coverage.

What documents are required to export from Turkey to the USA?

Standard documents include a commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading (sea) or airway bill (air), certificate of origin, and a US Customs Entry filing (ISF — Importer Security Filing — must be submitted 24 hours before loading for ocean freight). Certain goods require additional FDA, FCC or CPSC certifications.

Are there import duties on Turkish goods entering the USA?

The USA applies customs duties based on HS codes under the Harmonized Tariff Schedule. Some Turkish goods benefit from GSP (Generalized System of Preferences), which may reduce or eliminate duties. Rates vary significantly by product category. We advise on applicable duties before shipment.

What does air freight from Turkey to the USA cost?

Air freight rates from Turkey to the USA depend on weight, dimensions, the airport pair, and market conditions. Rates are typically quoted per kilogram with a minimum charge. Contact us for a current market rate — we respond within 1 hour.

Can you ship from the USA to Turkey as well?

Yes. We handle freight in both directions. US exports to Turkey include machinery, aircraft parts, agricultural products, chemicals and medical equipment. The same air and sea freight options apply for USA to Turkey shipments.
