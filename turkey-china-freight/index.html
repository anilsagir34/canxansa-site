THE MIDDLE CORRIDOR — RUSSIA-FREE RAIL FROM CHINA TO EUROPE

The Middle Corridor (Trans-Caspian International Transport Route) runs from China through Kazakhstan, across the Caspian Sea by ferry, through Azerbaijan and Georgia to Turkey — then onward to Europe by road. It bypasses Russia and Belarus entirely, a critical advantage since 2022 sanctions disrupted the northern rail route.

Transit from Shanghai to Istanbul: 12–18 days by rail. From Istanbul, goods continue to any European destination within 3–5 days by road. Canxansa manages the complete multimodal chain. Learn more about China to Europe via Turkey.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
CHINA — FAQ.

Air, rail or sea — which is right for my shipment?

Air is fastest (3–8 days door-to-door) but most expensive — suited for high-value or time-sensitive cargo. Rail via the Middle Corridor (12–18 days to Turkey, 16–26 days to Europe) offers a strong middle ground: faster than sea, significantly cheaper than air. Sea is the most economical for large volumes where transit time is not critical (14–38 days depending on destination).

What is the Middle Corridor and why use it?

The Middle Corridor (Trans-Caspian International Transport Route) runs from China through Kazakhstan, across the Caspian Sea by ferry, through Azerbaijan and Georgia to Turkey. It bypasses Russia and Belarus entirely — essential since 2022 sanctions disrupted the northern rail corridor. Transit from Shanghai or Chengdu to Istanbul: 12–18 days.

Do you handle door-to-door from Chinese factories?

Yes. We arrange pickup from your factory or supplier warehouse anywhere in China, manage Chinese export customs, handle international freight by air, sea or rail, clear import customs at the destination country, and deliver to the final address. One point of contact throughout.

Which ports and airports in China do you work with?

Sea: Shanghai (CNSHA), Ningbo (CNNGB), Guangzhou (CNCAN), Shenzhen (CNSZX), Tianjin (CNTXG). Air: Shanghai Pudong (PVG), Beijing (PEK), Guangzhou (CAN), Shenzhen (SZX). Rail (Middle Corridor): Chengdu, Xi'an, Zhengzhou and Yiwu connect to the Kazakhstan network westbound.

What export documents does a Chinese shipper need?

Standard export documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, HS code classification, Chinese export customs declaration, bill of lading or airway bill, and certificate of origin where preferential tariff is claimed at destination. Additional certificates (conformity, phytosanitary) may be required depending on the destination country and product type. We advise per shipment.

Is Turkey a transit hub for China to Europe shipments?

Yes. Istanbul is the western terminus of the Middle Corridor. Goods arriving by rail from China continue to any European destination within 3–5 days by road. Turkey can also serve as a processing or value-add hub — goods partially manufactured in Turkey may qualify for preferential origin when entering the EU under Turkey's Customs Union. See China to Europe via Turkey.

What import duties apply to Chinese goods in Turkey?

Turkey applies customs duties and anti-dumping measures on certain Chinese product categories — particularly textiles, ceramics, steel and chemicals. Applicable rates depend on the HS code. We advise on duty exposure and import compliance before shipment to avoid delays at customs clearance.
