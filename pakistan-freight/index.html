KARACHI & PORT QASIM — PAKISTAN'S GATEWAY TO THE WORLD

Karachi Port and Port Qasim handle the vast majority of Pakistan's seaborne trade, while Jinnah International (Karachi), Lahore, Islamabad and Sialkot airports connect the country's industrial clusters to global air-cargo networks. Together they make Pakistan a major export origin for textiles, leather, surgical instruments and agricultural goods bound for Turkey, Europe and the Gulf.

From Karachi, ocean freight reaches Turkey in 14–18 days, the UAE in 3–5 days and Northern Europe in 22–28 days. Air cargo from Karachi or Lahore reaches Istanbul in a 5–6 hour flight. For overland movement, the ECO Islamabad–Tehran–Istanbul (ITI) rail corridor offers a periodic alternative to the sea route. Canxansa manages end-to-end shipments from Pakistani factories and warehouses — customs, freight and last-mile delivery under a single point of contact.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
PAKISTAN — FAQ.

What is the fastest way to ship from Pakistan to Turkey?

Air freight from Karachi, Lahore or Islamabad to Istanbul is the fastest option — a flight time of around 5–6 hours, typically 1–2 days door-to-door. Sea freight from Karachi and Port Qasim to Mersin or Istanbul takes 14–18 days via the Suez Canal and is the most economical mode for large volumes. The ECO Islamabad–Tehran–Istanbul (ITI) rail corridor offers an overland alternative for suitable cargo.

How long does sea freight from Karachi take?

Karachi or Port Qasim to Mersin/Istanbul: 14–18 days via Suez. Karachi to Jebel Ali (UAE): 3–5 days. Karachi to Hamburg or Rotterdam: 22–28 days via Suez. Karachi to Shanghai: 18–24 days. Transit times depend on the shipping line's service loop and number of port calls. LCL consignments add 3–5 days for consolidation and deconsolidation.

Is there a rail freight option from Pakistan to Turkey?

Yes. The ECO Islamabad–Tehran–Istanbul (ITI) rail corridor links Pakistan to Turkey overland via Iran. It offers an alternative to the maritime route for suitable containerised cargo, with transit shorter than sea freight. Service runs are periodic rather than daily, so we confirm scheduling and feasibility for your shipment before booking.

Do Pakistani goods face customs duties in Turkey?

Turkey and Pakistan have a bilateral Trade in Goods Agreement that provides preferential tariff treatment for selected product lines. Coverage is limited to listed goods, so applicable duties depend on your specific HS classification. We advise on HS codes, certificates of origin and whether your cargo qualifies for preference before you ship.

Which ports and airports do you work with in Pakistan?

Sea: Karachi Port (PKKHI) and Port Qasim (PKBQM) — Pakistan's two main container gateways. Air: Jinnah International Karachi (KHI), Allama Iqbal Lahore (LHE), Islamabad International (ISB) and Sialkot International (SKT). We arrange pickup from any location in Pakistan, including the industrial clusters of Karachi, Lahore, Faisalabad and Sialkot.

What export documents does a Pakistani exporter need?

Standard export documents: commercial invoice, packing list, HS code classification, electronic export declaration via the WeBOC / Pakistan Single Window (PSW) system, bill of lading (sea) or airway bill (air), and certificate of origin. Textile and food shipments may require additional inspection or certification. We advise on documentation before shipment.

What goods are commonly exported from Pakistan?

Pakistan's leading exports include textiles and garments, cotton and home textiles, leather goods, surgical and medical instruments (Sialkot), sports goods, rice and agricultural products, and chemicals. High-value or time-sensitive cargo such as surgical instruments typically moves by air, while textiles and bulk goods move by sea.
