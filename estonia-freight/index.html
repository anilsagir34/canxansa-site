Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
ESTONIA — FAQ.

Why use an Estonia-registered freight forwarder?

CANXANSA OÜ is incorporated in Tallinn, Estonia (Registry: 17366254). As an EU-based company, we coordinate the full shipment chain under a single contract — carrier booking, documentation preparation and customs agent liaison for both export and import clearance. You get EU-compliant VAT invoicing and one point of contact throughout.

How long does road freight from Estonia to Turkey take?

TIR trucks from Tallinn to Istanbul take 4–6 days, routing south through Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Hungary, Serbia and Bulgaria. Transit to Ankara is 5–7 days, to Izmir 6–8 days. Both FTL (full truck) and LTL groupage are available. We run consolidated departures connecting Baltic cargo from Riga and Klaipeda through our Tallinn hub.

What sea freight options are available from Estonia?

Muuga Port (near Tallinn) is one of the largest cargo ports in the Baltic region. Sea freight to Turkey routes via Baltic–North Sea–Mediterranean, typically transhipping at Hamburg or Antwerp. Transit to Mersin or Istanbul: 14–20 days. LCL consolidation available for smaller consignments.

Does the EU-Turkey Customs Union apply to Estonian exports?

Yes. Industrial goods exported from Estonia to Turkey under the EU-Turkey Customs Union enter Turkey without customs duties, applicable to machinery, electronics, wood products, chemicals and food processing equipment, subject to rules of origin. We prepare EUR.1 movement certificates and all required origin documentation.

Can you consolidate cargo from Latvia and Lithuania?

Yes. We regularly consolidate shipments from Riga, Ventspils, Vilnius and Klaipeda through our Tallinn hub. Baltic groupage is available for LCL volumes — we pick up from your location in Latvia or Lithuania and combine with Estonian cargo for efficient southbound departures.

What documents are needed to export from Estonia?

Standard EU export documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, export customs declaration (EX-A), EUR.1 or origin declaration, and bill of lading (sea), airway bill (air) or CMR (road). We handle all documentation directly as an EU-registered entity — no third-party customs agent required.

Do you ship timber and oversized cargo from Estonia?

Yes. Timber, wood panels and other bulk or oversized cargo are regularly handled from Estonian and Baltic origins. Sea freight (FCL) is standard for large timber shipments. Road freight with flatbed or curtainsider trailers handles palletised and oversized loads.
