Air Freight

ISTANBUL TO EUROPE, BY AIR.

Published June 2026 Updated July 2026 Reading time ~6 min Hub IST / SAW

When a shipment absolutely has to be in Frankfurt on Thursday, the answer is air. Istanbul is one of the best places in the world to ship air cargo from: Istanbul Airport (IST) is among the largest cargo hubs anywhere, with Sabiha Gökçen (SAW), Izmir (ADB) and Ankara (ESB) adding regional capacity. This guide covers what exporters actually need to know — transit times, how pricing really works, documents and the cases where air freight earns its premium.

/ 01Why Istanbul is an air cargo power base

IST combines three things exporters care about: massive belly and freighter capacity, direct connections to 50+ European destinations plus the Gulf, Asia, Africa and the Americas, and a deep ground-handling ecosystem that keeps cut-offs late and recovery fast. For Turkish exporters — and for cargo transiting Turkey from Asia or the Middle East — it is the natural gateway.

/ 02Istanbul to Europe: when air is the right call

Europe is the corridor where the air decision needs the most thought. Road freight from Turkey reaches Central Europe in 3–5 days at a fraction of the air rate, so flying cargo to Europe is never the default — it is the tool for shipments that cannot absorb those days.

  • Fixed deadlines — a trade fair, a retail launch date, a vessel cut-off at a European port.
  • Stopped production — when a line in Germany or France is waiting on parts, a day saved is worth more than the entire freight bill.
  • High-value, low-volume cargo — electronics, instruments and spare parts, where inventory cost and transit risk dominate the equation.
  • The split shipment — fly the quantity the customer needs this week, send the balance by road.

With direct flights from Istanbul to 50+ European destinations, most consignments connect without transshipment — which is what keeps the 1–3 day door-to-door range realistic rather than theoretical.

Ranges cover the full chain — pickup, export clearance, flight, import clearance, delivery. The flight itself is hours; the schedule is set by clearance and connections. Minimum practical shipment size is around 45 kg; below that, courier services usually price better.

/ 04Chargeable weight: the rule that surprises everyone

Air freight is priced on chargeable weight — the greater of actual weight and volumetric weight. The standard IATA formula divides the volume in cubic centimetres by 6,000:

Example

A pallet of 120 × 100 × 100 cm = 1,200,000 cm³ ÷ 6,000 = 200 kg volumetric. If the actual weight is 130 kg, you pay for 200 kg. If it weighs 260 kg, you pay for 260 kg.

The practical consequence: light, bulky cargo is expensive by air. Dense cargo — machined parts, fasteners, electronics — flies efficiently. Before booking, give your forwarder real dimensions, not just weight; the quote depends on both.

/ 05What ships by air from Turkey

  • Time-critical production parts — a stopped line costs more per day than any air rate.
  • High-value goods — electronics and precision equipment, where fast transit cuts risk and inventory cost.
  • Perishables — fresh food and agricultural products with shelf-life economics.
  • Pharma and healthcare — temperature-managed, compliance-heavy cargo.
  • Samples and launch stock — textiles and apparel ahead of the season, trade-fair shipments, first orders that win the relationship.

/ 06Documents and process

The document set is lean: commercial invoice, packing list and the Air Waybill (AWB) issued when the shipment is booked, plus the Turkish export declaration and any product-specific certificates. Security screening is mandatory for all air cargo; shipments from unvetted shippers are screened before acceptance, which is built into the timeline.

  1. Booking — dimensions, weight, ready date; we confirm flight and rate.
  2. Pickup and export clearance — same-day or next-day in major Turkish industrial centres.
  3. Screening and acceptance at the airport, then uplift.
  4. Import clearance and delivery at destination — with the consignee's import details prepared in advance so the cargo doesn't sit.

/ 07Frequently asked questions

When does air freight actually pay off against road to Europe?
Road to Central Europe runs 3–5 days and costs far less — so for Europe, air wins only when one or two days matter, or for high-value cargo where transit risk and inventory cost dominate. For the Gulf, Asia, Africa and the Americas, air competes against 7–35 day sea transits, and the case is much easier.
Can I combine air with a cheaper mode?
Yes — the classic split: the quantity needed this week flies, the balance follows by road or sea. It is usually the cheapest way to meet a deadline without airfreighting the whole order.
Do you handle dangerous goods (DG) by air?
DG by air is heavily regulated (IATA DGR) — classification, packing, marking and documentation must be exact, and some classes cannot fly. Send us the MSDS and we confirm flyability and requirements before you commit.
Which is better, IST or SAW?
It depends on the airline and destination — IST has the deepest network; SAW often works well for specific carriers and for shippers on the Asian side of Istanbul. We book whichever serves your lane best; you don't need to choose.
