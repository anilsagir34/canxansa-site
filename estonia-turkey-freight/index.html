Common Questions

ESTONIA → TURKEY
FREIGHT FAQ.

Why use a dual-hub forwarder for Estonia–Turkey?

Most freight forwarders handle either the EU side or the Turkey side and rely on partner agents for the other end. We have direct operations in both — eliminating agent margins, communication delays and split liability. One contract, one invoice, one team.

How long does sea freight from Estonia to Turkey take?

Sea freight from Muuga port (Tallinn) to Mersin or Istanbul takes 14–20 days depending on the carrier and routing. Most shipments transit via Hamburg or Antwerp transhipment. FCL is direct; LCL may add 2–3 days for consolidation.

What documents are needed for Estonia–Turkey freight?

Standard EU export documents (commercial invoice, packing list, EX-A export declaration, EUR.1 movement certificate or supplier declaration for preferential origin under EU-Turkey Customs Union). Turkish import requires gümrük beyannamesi and applicable certificates.

Do you handle EU VAT and Turkish customs?

Yes. Our Estonian entity issues VAT-compliant invoices and handles EU export clearance directly. Our Turkish team manages import declaration, customs duty calculation and onward delivery. No third-party broker fees.

Can you ship from other Baltic countries (Latvia, Lithuania)?

Yes. We routinely consolidate cargo from Riga, Klaipeda, Vilnius and other Baltic origins through our Tallinn hub. Cross-Baltic groupage available for LCL volumes — contact us for combined Baltic-to-Turkey rates.

What is the EU-Turkey Customs Union benefit?

Under the EU-Turkey Customs Union (since 1995), most industrial goods qualify for duty-free movement between EU and Turkey — provided proper documentation (A.TR certificate). We prepare A.TR certificates as part of our standard service, saving you 5–20% on customs duties.
