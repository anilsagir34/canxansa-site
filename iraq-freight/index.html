HABUR & UMM QASR — IRAQ'S EXPORT GATEWAYS

Iraq's foreign trade runs through two principal gateways: the Habur (Ibrahim Khalil) land crossing in the north, the busiest commercial border between Iraq and Turkey and the start of the overland corridor to Europe; and Umm Qasr in the south, Iraq's main deep-water container port on the Gulf, alongside the industrial port of Khor al-Zubair. Together with the airports at Baghdad, Erbil and Basra, they connect Iraq's energy, agricultural and trading base to every major market.

From Iraq, road freight reaches Turkey in 3–5 days via Habur and continues to Europe overland in 7–10 days; sea freight from Umm Qasr reaches the Gulf in 4–7 days, India in 7–12 days and the Far East in 20–28 days; and air cargo from Baghdad and Erbil reaches Istanbul and the European hubs in about a day. Canxansa manages end-to-end shipments from Iraqi cities, ports and industrial sites — customs, freight and last-mile delivery under a single point of contact.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
IRAQ — FAQ.

What is the fastest way to ship from Iraq to Turkey and Europe?

Air freight from Baghdad International (BGW) or Erbil International (EBL) reaches Istanbul in about 1–2 days and the main European hubs in roughly a day, ideal for high-value and time-critical cargo. For volume, road freight via the Habur (Ibrahim Khalil) crossing reaches Turkey in 3–5 days and continues to Europe overland in 7–10 days, while sea freight from Umm Qasr runs to Turkey and the Mediterranean via the Gulf and Suez. Transit times are indicative and depend on border queues, the carrier's service loop and routing.

How does road freight from Iraq to Turkey work?

Trucks run from Baghdad, Erbil, Mosul and Sulaymaniyah north to the Habur (Ibrahim Khalil) border gate — the busiest commercial crossing between Iraq and Turkey — and onward into Turkey and Europe. Transit to Turkey is typically 3–5 days depending on border queue times. Both full truckload (FTL) and groupage (LTL) are available, and the corridor also serves as a transit route for cargo moving between the Gulf and Europe.

How long does sea freight from Iraq take?

Indicative from Umm Qasr / Khor al-Zubair: Iraq to the UAE (Jebel Ali) 4–7 days; Iraq to Saudi Arabia (Dammam) 3–6 days; Iraq to India (Nhava Sheva/Mundra) 7–12 days; Iraq to Singapore 14–18 days; Iraq to China (Shanghai) 20–28 days; Iraq to Turkey and the Mediterranean 14–20 days; and to North Europe 24–30 days via the Gulf and Suez. FCL (20'/40') and LCL groupage are both available. LCL adds 3–5 days for consolidation and deconsolidation.

Which ports, airports and borders do you work with in Iraq?

Sea: Umm Qasr (IQUQR), Iraq's main deep-water container gateway on the Gulf, and the adjacent industrial port of Khor al-Zubair (IQKAZ). Air: Baghdad International (BGW), Erbil International (EBL) and Basra International (BSR). Road: the Habur (Ibrahim Khalil) crossing into Turkey, the primary commercial land gateway. We arrange pickup across Iraq, including Baghdad, Basra, Erbil, Mosul and Sulaymaniyah.

Can freight from Iraq continue to the Gulf and Europe overland?

Yes. The Habur corridor links Iraq with Turkey and onward to Europe, while road services southward reach Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, the UAE and Qatar. As regional routing has shifted, the Iraq–Turkey overland corridor has seen increased use as an alternative gateway between the Gulf and Europe. We arrange door-to-door delivery in both directions with customs handled at the border.

What export documents does an Iraqi exporter need?

Standard documents: commercial invoice, packing list, the transport document — bill of lading (sea), airway bill (air) or CMR (road) — the Iraqi customs export declaration, and a certificate of origin where the destination requires it. Iraq applies specific controls on food, chemicals, petroleum products and construction materials. We advise on full documentation before shipment to avoid border and port delays.

What goods are commonly shipped from Iraq?

Beyond crude oil, Iraq's freight base includes petroleum products and derivatives, dates and agricultural produce, scrap and raw materials, and re-export and project cargo tied to reconstruction and the energy sector. High-value and time-sensitive lines move by air; volume cargo moves by sea FCL from Umm Qasr and by road via Habur for the Turkey and Europe corridors.
