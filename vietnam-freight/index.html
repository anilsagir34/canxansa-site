CAI MEP & CAT LAI — VIETNAM'S MANUFACTURING EXPORT ENGINE

Vietnam ships through the deepwater Cai Mep and the busy Cat Lai terminal near Ho Chi Minh City — with direct services to the US and Europe — together with Hai Phong serving the northern electronics belt around Hanoi, and Da Nang in the centre. Air gateways at Tan Son Nhat (SGN) and Noi Bai (HAN) move high-value electronics and garments. Together they connect Vietnam's fast-growing manufacturing base — electronics, textiles, footwear and furniture — to every major market.

From Vietnam, transpacific sailings reach the US West Coast in 18–24 days; intra-Asia services reach Singapore in 2–4 days, China in 3–6 days and Korea and Japan within a week; air cargo reaches the US and European hubs in a day or two; and sea freight to Europe and Turkey runs 24–32 days via Suez. Canxansa manages end-to-end shipments from Vietnamese factories and industrial parks — customs, freight and last-mile delivery under a single point of contact.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
VIETNAM — FAQ.

What is the fastest way to ship from Vietnam to the USA and Europe?

Air freight from Ho Chi Minh (SGN) or Hanoi (HAN) reaches the US and the main European hubs in about a day or two door-to-door — ideal for electronics, garments and time-critical cargo. Sea freight is the most economical mode for volume: Cai Mep to the US West Coast runs 18–24 days transpacific, and to North Europe (Rotterdam, Hamburg) 26–32 days via Suez. Transit times are indicative and depend on the carrier's service loop and routing.

How long does sea freight from Vietnam's ports take?

Indicative from Cai Mep / Cat Lai / Hai Phong: China 3–6 days; Singapore 2–4 days; South Korea 4–7 days; Japan 5–9 days; the US West Coast 18–24 days transpacific; India 10–16 days; the UAE 16–22 days; North Europe and Turkey 24–32 days via Suez. FCL (20'/40'), reefer and LCL groupage are all available. LCL adds 3–5 days for consolidation and deconsolidation.

Can you ship intra-Asia from Vietnam to China, Korea, Japan and Singapore?

Yes. Intra-Asia is one of Vietnam's busiest export lanes. Short-sea services reach Singapore in 2–4 days, China in 3–6 days, Korea in 4–7 days and Japan in 5–9 days, with frequent sailings and daily air options. We handle the customs formalities at both ends and consolidate cargo for fast regional distribution.

Which ports and airports do you work with in Vietnam?

Sea: Cat Lai (VNSGN) and the deepwater Cai Mep (VNCMT) near Ho Chi Minh City — with direct US and EU services — Hai Phong (VNHPH) in the north and Da Nang (VNDAD) in the centre. Air: Ho Chi Minh Tan Son Nhat (SGN) and Hanoi Noi Bai (HAN). We arrange pickup from any region, including the southern and northern industrial and electronics belts.

Do you handle electronics, garment and refrigerated agricultural exports?

Yes. Electronics and phones, garments and footwear, and furniture are among Vietnam's leading exports — we move them by air for time-critical lines and by sea FCL/LCL for volume. We also handle refrigerated agriculture and seafood — coffee, cashews, pepper, rice and shrimp — in reefer containers, with the phytosanitary and sanitary documentation destinations require.

What export documents does a Vietnamese exporter need?

Standard documents: commercial invoice, packing list, the customs export declaration through VNACCS, the transport document — bill of lading (sea) or airway bill (air) — and a certificate of origin. Vietnam has a wide FTA network (EVFTA with the EU, CPTPP, ASEAN/ATIGA, UKVFTA and others), so a preferential origin certificate (e.g. EUR.1 / Form E / Form D) can reduce duties. We advise on documentation before shipment.

What goods are commonly exported from Vietnam?

Leading Vietnamese exports include electronics and phones, garments and textiles, footwear, furniture and wood products, and agricultural goods and seafood (coffee, rice, cashews, pepper, shrimp). High-value and time-sensitive cargo moves by air, while containerised and reefer consignments move by sea from Cai Mep, Cat Lai and Hai Phong.
