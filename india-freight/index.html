INDIA TO EUROPE — DIRECT SEA ROUTES VIA SUEZ AND THE EMERGING IMEC CORRIDOR

India is the world's fifth-largest economy and one of the fastest-growing export nations. Pharmaceuticals, textiles, engineering goods, IT hardware and gems move in volume from Indian ports to European buyers every month. The primary freight route from India to Europe runs via the Suez Canal — 18–25 days by sea from Mumbai or Chennai to Turkish and Mediterranean ports. From Turkey, goods continue to any European destination within 3–5 days by road.

The India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC), announced at the G20 Summit in September 2023, is designed to connect India to Europe via the UAE, Saudi Arabia and Jordan through a combined sea-rail route. When fully operational, it will offer transit times faster than the all-sea Suez route. Canxansa operates across Turkey and the Middle East — positioned along the approach corridors of this emerging trade route. Learn more about Turkey-India bilateral freight.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
INDIA — FAQ.

What are the main freight routes from India to Europe?

The primary route is sea freight via the Suez Canal: 18–25 days from JNPT Mumbai or Chennai to Turkish and Mediterranean ports, and 28–38 days to Northern European ports such as Rotterdam and Hamburg. Air freight is available from Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai and Bangalore — 6–9 days to European destinations. For cost-optimised speed, multimodal solutions (sea to UAE, then air or road onward) offer a middle ground.

How long does sea freight from India to Turkey take?

FCL from JNPT Mumbai or Mundra to Mersin or Istanbul takes 18–25 days via the Suez Canal, depending on the shipping line's schedule and port calls. LCL (consolidated) takes 22–30 days due to additional consolidation and de-consolidation time at origin and destination. Transit times from Chennai are similar — the port is slightly further from the Suez approach route, adding 1–2 days in some cases.

What is the IMEC corridor and how does it affect India freight?

The India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC) was announced at the G20 Summit in September 2023. It proposes a combined sea-rail trade route from India to Europe via the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Jordan and the Mediterranean — bypassing the Suez Canal for rail portions. The corridor is still in the planning and early infrastructure phase. When operational, it will offer faster transit than all-sea Suez routing. Canxansa operates in the Middle East and Turkey and is positioned to serve this corridor once it becomes active.

Do you handle door-to-door from Indian factories?

Yes. We arrange pickup from your factory, warehouse or supplier anywhere in India, handle Indian export customs clearance, manage ocean or air freight, clear import customs at the destination country and deliver to the final address. One point of contact throughout — no need to coordinate multiple local agents.

Which ports and airports in India do you work with?

Sea: JNPT Nhava Sheva (INNSA), Mundra (INMUN), Chennai (INMAA), Kolkata (INCCU), Vizag (INVTZ). Air: Mumbai (BOM), Delhi (DEL), Chennai (MAA), Bangalore (BLR), Hyderabad (HYD). We coordinate pickup from all major manufacturing zones including Surat, Tirupur, Ludhiana, Pune and the National Capital Region.

What export documents does an Indian exporter need?

Standard export documents: commercial invoice, packing list, HS code classification, shipping bill (Indian export customs declaration), bill of lading or airway bill, and certificate of origin where preferential tariff treatment is claimed at destination. Pharmaceuticals require additional documentation — drug regulatory certificates, GMP compliance records and country-specific import approvals. We advise per product and destination.

Is Turkey a transit point for India-Europe shipments?

Turkey sits at the western end of the Suez approach corridor and serves as a natural gateway into Europe. Goods arriving by sea from India at Mersin or Istanbul can continue to European destinations by road within 3–5 days. Turkey's Customs Union with the EU also creates value-add opportunities — Indian goods processed or partially manufactured in Turkey may benefit from preferential tariff treatment when entering the EU. See Turkey-India freight.
