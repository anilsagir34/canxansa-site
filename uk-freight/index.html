Since the UK left the EU's single market in January 2021, all commercial shipments between the UK and EU countries — and between the UK and Turkey — require customs declarations in both directions. This applies to road freight via the Channel Tunnel or ferry, sea freight, and air cargo.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
THE UK — FAQ.

What changed for UK freight after Brexit?

Since January 2021, the UK is outside the EU's single market and customs union. All shipments between the UK and EU countries now require customs declarations in both directions — including export declarations at origin and import declarations at destination. UK exporters need an EORI number, commodity codes (HS codes) and a commercial invoice for every international shipment. We handle customs compliance end-to-end.

How long does road freight from the UK to Turkey take?

TIR trucks from the UK to Istanbul take 4–6 days, routing via the Channel Tunnel or ferry to France, then through the Balkans (Serbia–Bulgaria–Turkey). To Ankara or Izmir, allow 5–7 days. We offer both FTL and LTL groupage options.

What are the main sea freight options from UK ports?

Felixstowe is the UK's largest container port, serving global routes. Southampton handles both deep-sea and short-sea services. London Gateway is a major deep-sea hub. For Turkey, transit time is 10–14 days via Suez or Mediterranean routing. For the Middle East, 18–26 days. LCL consolidation is available.

Do I need a customs agent for UK exports now?

Yes, for all commercial shipments leaving or entering the UK since Brexit. We act as your customs broker — handling export declarations on the UK side, coordinating with agents at the destination country, and advising on HS codes, duty rates and required certificates. This applies to EU destinations as well as Turkey and the Middle East.

Does the UK have a free trade agreement with Turkey?

Yes. The UK-Turkey Trade Agreement, signed in December 2020, replicates much of the former EU-Turkey Customs Union for industrial goods. British exporters of manufactured products — machinery, automotive parts, chemicals, textiles — benefit from reduced or zero tariffs when exporting to Turkey, provided they meet rules of origin requirements and present the relevant documentation.

Can you ship from Edinburgh, Birmingham or other UK cities?

Yes. We arrange collection from anywhere in the UK — London, Birmingham, Manchester, Glasgow, Edinburgh, Bristol, Leeds and beyond. For air freight we use the nearest major hub airport. For road and sea we route via the most efficient UK port for your cargo size and destination.

Do you handle dangerous goods or hazardous chemicals from the UK?

Yes, subject to correct classification and packaging. Dangerous goods shipments require proper UN classification, MSDS/SDS sheets, and appropriate labelling per IATA (air), IMDG (sea) or ADR (road) regulations. We advise on classification and arrange compliant shipment.
