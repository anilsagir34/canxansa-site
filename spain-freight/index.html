ALGECIRAS — EUROPE'S GATEWAY TO THE MEDITERRANEAN AND BEYOND

The Port of Algeciras is one of Europe's busiest container hubs — strategically positioned at the Strait of Gibraltar where the Atlantic meets the Mediterranean. It handles over 5 million TEUs annually and offers direct connections to Turkey, the Middle East, Asia and the Americas. For Spanish exporters, Algeciras provides faster Mediterranean routing and competitive rates compared to Atlantic-only options.

Barcelona and Valencia serve as the primary Mediterranean ports for eastern and northern Spain, with direct feeder services to Istanbul and Mersin.

Common Questions

FREIGHT FROM
SPAIN — FAQ.

How long does road freight from Spain to Turkey take?

TIR trucks from Spain to Istanbul take 4–6 days, routing through France and then via the Balkan corridor (Serbia–Bulgaria–Turkey). Transit to Izmir is 5–7 days, to Ankara 5–7 days. We offer both FTL (full truck) and LTL groupage options depending on your cargo volume.

What are the main sea freight routes from Spain to Turkey?

Barcelona and Valencia are the primary departure ports for Mediterranean routing to Istanbul or Mersin — transit 8–12 days. Algeciras, positioned at the Strait of Gibraltar, offers feeder connections to major Mediterranean carriers with competitive rates. LCL consolidation is available from all three ports.

Does the EU-Turkey Customs Union apply to Spanish exports?

Yes. Industrial goods exported from Spain to Turkey under the EU-Turkey Customs Union enter Turkey without customs duties — applicable to machinery, automotive parts, chemicals, textiles and electronics, subject to rules of origin. Agricultural products fall outside the Customs Union. We prepare EUR.1 certificates and origin documentation for all eligible shipments.

Can you handle fresh produce and temperature-sensitive cargo?

Yes. We manage temperature-controlled shipments including citrus, vegetables, olive oil and other Spanish agricultural exports. These require specific documentation (phytosanitary certificates, health certificates) and appropriate cold-chain handling. Air freight is available for perishable cargo requiring fast delivery.

What documents are required to export from Spain?

Standard export documentation: commercial invoice, packing list, Spanish export customs declaration (EX-A), EUR.1 or origin declaration for preferential tariff, and bill of lading (sea), airway bill (air) or CMR (road). Phytosanitary and health certificates are required for food products. We coordinate all documentation.

Do you ship from Barcelona, Madrid and other Spanish cities?

Yes. We collect cargo from anywhere in Spain — Barcelona, Madrid, Valencia, Seville, Bilbao, Zaragoza and beyond. For air freight we primarily use BCN and MAD. For sea, we route via the nearest appropriate port based on your location and destination.

Can you handle ceramic tiles and heavy industrial cargo?

Yes. Spain is a leading exporter of ceramic tiles and we regularly handle heavyweight, palletised cargo. Sea freight (FCL or LCL) is the standard mode. We advise on container loading, blocking and bracing, and packaging for fragile goods.
