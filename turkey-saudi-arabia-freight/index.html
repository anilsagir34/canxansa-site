Common Questions

FREIGHT FAQ.

How long does sea freight from Turkey to Saudi Arabia take?

Sea freight from Istanbul or Mersin to Jeddah typically takes 14–20 days. Dammam (on the Gulf side) may take slightly longer depending on routing. Transit times vary with carrier selection and seasonal port congestion.

What documents are required to ship to Saudi Arabia?

Saudi Arabia has strict customs requirements. Key documents include commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin (attested by the Saudi Embassy or Chamber of Commerce), bill of lading or airway bill, and an import permit for regulated goods. We guide you through the full documentation process.

Which Saudi port is best for my shipment?

For goods destined to western Saudi Arabia and Riyadh, Jeddah Islamic Port is the standard choice. For the Eastern Province, Jubail or Dammam (King Abdulaziz Port) is more appropriate. We recommend the right port based on your final delivery address to minimise inland transport costs.

Are there any restricted goods for Turkey-Saudi Arabia shipments?

Saudi Arabia restricts or prohibits various goods including alcohol, pork products, certain chemicals and items deemed contrary to Islamic law. We advise on commodity-specific requirements and ensure HS codes and documentation are prepared correctly before shipment.

Do you handle customs clearance in Saudi Arabia?

Yes. We work with licensed Saudi customs brokers to manage import clearance, VAT (15% ZATCA) and applicable duties. We ensure all documentation is complete and compliant before cargo departs Turkey to avoid delays on arrival.
